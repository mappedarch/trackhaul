TRACKHAUL FLEET OPERATIONS
Fuel Anomaly Detection Reference Guide

NORMAL FUEL CONSUMPTION BASELINES
Vehicle Type: Heavy HGV (>20 tonnes)
- Motorway cruising: 28-32 litres per 100km
- Urban delivery: 38-45 litres per 100km
- Mixed route: 33-38 litres per 100km

Vehicle Type: Medium HGV (7.5-20 tonnes)
- Motorway cruising: 18-22 litres per 100km
- Urban delivery: 25-30 litres per 100km
- Mixed route: 20-25 litres per 100km

ANOMALY THRESHOLDS
Minor anomaly: consumption exceeds baseline by 15-25%
Major anomaly: consumption exceeds baseline by more than 25%
Critical anomaly: consumption exceeds baseline by more than 40% or sudden drop indicating possible fuel theft

COMMON CAUSES OF FUEL ANOMALIES
1. Tyre pressure below specification
   - Impact: up to 3% increased consumption per 10 kPa below specification
   - Resolution: check and inflate all tyres to manufacturer specification

2. Excessive idling
   - Impact: 2-4 litres per hour at idle
   - Threshold: flag vehicles idling more than 20% of engine-on time
   - Resolution: driver coaching, automatic engine cutoff after 10 minutes idle

3. Aggressive driving behaviour
   - Hard acceleration events above 2.5 m/s2 increase consumption by up to 20%
   - Resolution: driver coaching, review telematics score

4. AdBlue system fault
   - Symptoms: increased diesel consumption, DEF warning light
   - Resolution: inspect AdBlue dosing unit and NOx sensors

5. Fuel theft
   - Pattern: sudden unexplained drop in fuel level not correlated with distance
   - Threshold: drop of more than 20 litres not correlated with fuelling event
   - Resolution: immediate investigation, cross-reference with GPS stop data

INVESTIGATION WORKFLOW
Step 1: Confirm anomaly persists over minimum 3 consecutive trips
Step 2: Check maintenance history for open faults
Step 3: Review driver behaviour telematics for same period
Step 4: Cross-reference with fuel card transaction records
Step 5: Escalate to fleet engineer if no cause identified within 48 hours

REPORTING
All major and critical anomalies must be logged in the fleet management system
Monthly fuel efficiency report submitted to operations director
Vehicles with recurring anomalies flagged for scheduled inspection
